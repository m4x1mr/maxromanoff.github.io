Academy Sports + Outdoors

A Mobile Shopping App for a Retail Giant

Academy Sports + Outdoors is one of the largest sports and outdoors retailers in the world, raking in $5.7B in 2020. With the COVID-19 pandemic keeping many consumers at home, online sales have been skyrocketing for many companies, including Academy. As such, we worked to deliver a mobile app to grow and continue to drive value to their consumer base.

My role throughout the project

While working with Academy, I helped with designing the UX throughout the app. Though I worked with a team on defining the architecture, user flows, and designing the visuals, one of my responsibilities was also owning the usability testing aspect.

Timeline

Nov 2020 - Apr 2021
6 months

The initial release for the app was an MVP, so we worked closely with the lead designer at Academy to ensure the experience closely mirrored their website’s capabilities. Despite that, the Sapient team pushed for a few app specific features to drive engagement, over the website.

Curbside pick up & buy online, pick up in-store

How might we help Convenience Chasers receive their products faster?

As part of Academy's initial research that they brought to us, we knew that Convenience Chasers were a large segment. As such, we had to prioritize getting online-purchased products in the hands of this group, faster. Working with our product team, we found that delivering a seamless curbside and buy online, pick up in store experience was imperative. I worked with the lead designer from Academy to understand the current state of picking up online orders, both curbside and in-store, and designed the flows for both of these experiences. I also worked on designing the Apple wallet card that a user could scan to verify their purchase.

Barcode scanning

How might we help Value Verifiers learn more about in-store products?

Though shopping in-store has its benefits, there are drawbacks such as not knowing how a product is reviewed or if there are any deals going on. This is a very real problem to the Value Verifier segment which looks to get the most bang-for-their-buck. One way of solving such an issue is bringing the online-store with them, and thus allowing users to scan a product’s barcode and see more details about it online. I worked on the layout for the product description page and collaborated with another designer on the flow for this use case.

Omni-channel purchases

How might we help Passion Pursuers keep track of all of their purchases?

Being a segment that purchases only the best of the best, Passion Pursuers need the flexibility to return items, in case things don’t end up going as planned. Having to keep track of paper receipts, website purchases, and orders on an app could be complicated, so we designed a way to keep all orders in a single place, under a user’s account. Though this feature was a larger team effort, I worked on the flows for a user adding a store receipt to their account, as well as the order details pages.

After the hard work from the various teams involved, the app was launched in July 2021. Despite zero marketing spend to promote it, the app received numerous 5 star reviews, thousands of downloads, and tens of thousands in revenue, in just a few weeks.

What I learned

I was exposed to consumer research, design research, UI design, marketing, business, and video editing all while working in a startup environment for 4 months. This was the first time I've been exposed to Lean Startup within the workplace and working so quickly and iteratively. It was also exciting working with so many people higher up in the organization and seeing how many different teams this type of project brings together.

Before working on this team, this is something I would’ve never expected from a large company. Being able to basically “wing-it” when it came to our methods of problem solving compared to having a systemic way of going about a process is refreshing. I also loved the fact that everyday was spent working on something different. One day I could be doing only UI, and the next, I could be working on strategy and help identify issues that could seriously affect the final product. It definitely wasn't dull, and gets me excited to work in something mixed in strategy and design.
