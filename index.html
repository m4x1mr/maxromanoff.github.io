Latest thinking

My approach to design iterates as I learn. Rather than following a fixed process, I adapt based on the industry, scale, and urgency. Here are some principles that have been shaping how I approach problems recently.

Designing for root causes

It’s easy (and fast) to ship solutions that solve symptoms. I work with Product teams to question whether we’re addressing a symptom or if immediately solving the root cause could be more fruitful.

Edge cases impact craft

Certain problems require extra attention. I love exploring edge cases to make sure large, high-impact systems can scale appropriately, and to avoid costly rework later on.

Progress with subtraction

Design shouldn't just be about adding new features. Consolidation, removal, and simplification are often more impactful, especially across legacy platforms and systems.
